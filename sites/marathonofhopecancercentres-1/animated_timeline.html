2017

PILOT PHASE UNDERWAY

The Terry Fox Research Institute is implementing a sequential number of pilot projects to develop precision medicine programs and systems for sharing data. The first of these pilots was launched in April 2017. Working with BC Cancer and the Princess Margaret Cancer Centre, this pilot has a $12-million budget over 2 years. The self-funded pilot project is focused upon clinical genomics, immunotherapy, imaging and information technology linkages. The second pilot will develop a Montreal, Quebec Cancer Consortium with four major hospitals and two universities, which will be reviewed and launched in the first half of 2018. The third and fourth pilots will follow in the Atlantic and Prairie provinces with launches in late 2018 and 2019

2018

MARATHON OF HOPE CANCER CENTRE - DESIGNATION PROCESS RELEASED

The Terry Fox Research Institute intends to launch its designation program for Cancer Centres in 2018. The designation process will be published, and Centres will be invited to apply based on their capacity and performance in precision oncology as evaluated by international peers. The Terry Fox Research Institute intends to establish at least 5 Marathon of Hope Cancer Centres or Consortia across Canada.

2018 - 2020

MARATHON OF HOPE CANCER CENTRE - OFFICIAL DESIGNATIONS

Working in collaboration with its partners to apply the learnings and outcomes from the pilots, including technology solutions and best practices for data sharing, the Terry Fox Research Institute will designate the first Marathon of Hope Cancer Centres in 2018 and 2019. It is anticipated that these Centres will be in British Columbia, Ontario and Quebec. The Terry Fox Research Institute will designate Marathon of Hope Cancer Consortia in the Atlantic and Prairie provinces in 2019 and 2020. In addition, the Terry Fox Research Institute will seek to designate a Marathon of Hope program in pediatric cancers.
